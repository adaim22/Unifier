Code of Conduct

  • I. Public Interest

    a. Have due regard for public health, safety, privacy, security, and wellbeing of others.

    b. Have due regard for the rights of Third Parties.

    c. Don't discriminate on any grounds.

    d. Promote equal access to the benefits of computer science.


    II. Professional Competence & Integrity

    a. Only undertake what you are competent enough to accomplish

    b. Don't lie about your level of competence

    c. Always develop yourself professional and maintain knowledge of technological and methodological developments

    d. Ensure that you understand any revelant Legislation and comply with it

    e. Respect, value, and seek alternative viewpoints and honest criticism.

    f. Avoid injuring others or their property, reputation or employment through false, malicious, or negligent action, or inaction


    III. Duty to Relevant Authority

    a. Carry out your professional responsabilities with due care and dilligence

    b. Avoid any situation which could cause a conflict of interest

    c. Accept professional responsability for your own work and the work of any colleagues under your supervision

    d. Don't disclose or authorise to be disclosed confidential information for personal gain or for the benefit of a third part unless required to do so by Legislation or a relevant Authority

    e. Dont misrepresent or withhold information about the performance of products, systems, or services (unless lawfully bound by a duty to not disclose such information) to take advantage of the lack of knowledge of others


    IV. Duty to the Profession

    a. Accept your personal duty to uphold the reputation of the profession. Don't undertake actions which bring the profession into disrepure.

    b. Seek to improve professional standards through participation in their development, use, and enforcement.

    c. Encourage and support fellow members in their professional development
